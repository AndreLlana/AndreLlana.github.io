Care Team Checklists
Morning Shift
  • Wake-up time varies, but no later than 10 am.
  • Wake-up routine:
    • CPAP machine (turn off machine, check water level and refill with distilled water to ‘max line’ as needed). For more information on using CPAP machines, visit Sleep Foundation.
    • Glasses are kept in the tub on her TV tray near her bed – please clean with lens wipes or soap and water.
    • Help with clothes change; she prefers soft cotton fabrics.
    • She needs to wear compression socks up to her knee (can wear socks on top) and her Velcro shoes.
    • Empty bedside urinals.
    • Use toilet.
    • At the recliner, she will need her compression waist belt on (secure it tightly around her mid-section, not too high).
    • Put on headphones for hearing and hat and blanket.
    • Recliner is electric so can go up and down using remote attached to the TV tray.
  • Breakfast at 10:30 am.
  • Blood pressure: There is an automatic cuff on the table near the recliner. Please document blood pressure on the sheet near her recliner. You can learn more about blood pressure monitoring from American Heart Association.
  • Medication: Morning pills from the container; she has a weekly pill organizer. You can find tips for organizing medications at CDC.
  • Please have her use the toilet 15 minutes before leaving.
  • Please provide water and snacks before leaving.
  • Favorites: Cottage cheese with fruit, macaroni salad, apple sauce, hard-boiled egg, jello, pudding, tapioca, smoothies, cereal bar, Ensure drink, diced fruit.
Evening Shift
  • Dinner about 6:15 pm; she prefers her meals seasoned lightly.
  • Medication: Evening pills from the container around 7:30-8 pm; ensure she has a glass of water with them.
  • Light cleaning: Wipe counters after dinner, swifter, empty trash as needed.
  • Bedtime routine:
    • Use toilet.
    • Teeth brushing/mouthwash. For tips on oral hygiene, check out American Dental Association.
    • May want 2 puffs of her inhaler if short of breath once she is at her bed. For information on inhaler use, see American Lung Association.
    • In bed:
      • Remove glasses.
      • Apply lip balm.
      • Fresh water nearby.
      • Help with CPAP machine.
      • Make sure urinals and walker are close for overnight.
      • For a visual guide, check the video on preparing the CPAP machine:
    • Sleeps with her bedroom door open; she enjoys the sound of nature at night.
Sundays 
  • Refill medications for the week; she prefers to have her medications organized by day.
  • Wash and change sheets; she likes lavender-scented detergent.
  • Laundry; she prefers to have her clothes hung to dry when possible. For tips on laundry care, refer to Good Housekeeping.
  • Charge life alert (at desk); make sure it's functional. More info on life alerts can be found at Life Alert.
  • Charge iPad (at desk); she likes to watch her favorite shows on weekends.
  • Organize her favorite books for the week; she loves mystery novels.
  • Prepare a small treat for her weekly snack, like cookies or fruit.
