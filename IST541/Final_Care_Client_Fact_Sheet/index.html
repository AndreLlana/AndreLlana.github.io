Nancy's History
About
  • She grew up hiking the majestic trails of Colorado and spent her twenties exploring the national parks across the United States. She's called Santa Cruz her home for over 30 years.

  • She’s a warm conversationalist but prefers meaningful dialogue over small talk.

  • Her hearing is significantly diminished, with about 30% in her right ear and none in her left.

  • To amplify sounds, she uses a specialized audio device with headphones to assist her in hearing conversations and the television.

  • Speak clearly and slowly, as she often relies on lip-reading to understand speech.
Abilities
  • She can get up from her recliner to a stand with her walker on her own (chair has an automatic lift as needed).

  • She can walk to the toilet, her room, and kitchen with the walker. She could use reminders to pick up her feet.

  • No lifting should be required, but monitoring for safety, and she may need reminders to reach back when sitting down.

  • In bed, she can go from lying down to sitting at the bedside and then standing with the walker.

  • She fatigues easily and sometimes quickly, so pay attention to her breathing and legs/feet as she is moving.

  • She can use her albuterol inhaler as needed throughout the day (8 puffs/day).

  • She is a fall risk, so please be around to ‘standby’ if she is walking so that you can ask her to sit down if needed.
Activities
  • If she is feeling good energy-wise, she may like to do a little light painting or maybe go outside and do a few minutes of sitting in the sun or watering the plants with the watering can, but she mostly enjoys watching TV.

  • She loves her dog, Sophie, and Sophie stays near her most of the day. Learn more about pet care for seniors.
Frustrations
  • If asked too many questions and she can’t hear, she can get overstimulated and frustrated.

  • If her belongings are moved around the house without communicating where they are moved to.
The House
  • Two other housemates live in the house. Tommy is a caregiver tenant (room near bathroom) and Mitchel is a retired engineer and does not help with care routines.

  • Spare Key: Located at the front of the house under a cement brick near the fountain.

  • Please leave the front door unlocked when leaving morning shift (it has a turn style lock from the inside).

  • Garbage night is Sunday for a Monday morning pick-up.

  • Laundry is off of the kitchen and can be utilized after 10 am.

  • Please do not access the garage storage area or backyard unless discussed with housemates.
